intro: "Gas is one of the most consistent spending categories in a household budget. The average American household spends between $1,800 and $2,400 per year on gasoline, depending on vehicle type and commute distance. That is a meaningful category to optimize, and several credit cards earn at 3% to 5% specifically at gas stations.

The terminology matters here. Credit cards distinguish between gas stations (the physical pump, MCC code 5541 and 5542) and gas purchased at warehouse clubs like Costco or Sam's Club (which has a different merchant code). Some cards earn bonus rates at traditional gas stations but not at Costco's pumps, and vice versa. If you primarily fill up at Costco, that distinction matters significantly.

The other variable is annual earning caps. Cards that offer 5% at gas often cap that earning at $1,500 to $6,000 per year. At $150 per month in gas spending, that is $1,800 per year, so some caps may not bind, but heavy drivers should check.

Who benefits most from a gas rewards card? Commuters who drive frequently, families with multiple vehicles, anyone with a longer commute, and small businesses where fuel is a significant operating cost. At $2,000 per year in gas spending, the difference between 1% and 4% is $60 per year at zero cost, or more with a card that earns 5%."

faqs:
  - q: "Do gas credit cards work at Costco and Sam's Club gas pumps?"
    a: "It depends on the card. The Costco Anywhere Visa specifically earns 4% at Costco pumps. Many other gas credit cards do not earn at warehouse club gas stations because Costco and Sam's Club pumps often code as wholesale clubs (MCC 5300) rather than gas stations (MCC 5541). Check your card's terms if you primarily fill up at warehouse clubs."
  - q: "What is the gas merchant category code?"
    a: "Traditional gas stations and automated fuel dispensers use MCC codes 5541 and 5542. These are the codes that trigger bonus rewards on most gas credit cards. Warehouse club pumps like Costco (MCC 5300) and Amazon Fresh stations may not qualify. If you are unsure whether a station qualifies, the charge will still appear on your statement; check if it earned the bonus rate or the base rate."
  - q: "Does paying for gas at the pump earn the same rewards as paying inside?"
    a: "Usually yes, as long as it is the same merchant. Paying at the pump and paying inside at a gas station that also sells convenience items both code to the gas station MCC. The only exception is if the gas station is attached to a grocery store or big box retailer where the payment terminal codes to the larger store's MCC rather than the gas station."
  - q: "What is EV charging for credit card purposes?"
    a: "Several cards now explicitly include electric vehicle charging stations in their gas rewards categories.
