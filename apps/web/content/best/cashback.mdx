faqs:
  - q: "Is cashback better than travel rewards?"
    a: "For most people, yes. Travel points can be worth more per dollar if you redeem them for premium cabin flights at the right time, but the average person redeems miles at well below stated value. Cashback is always worth face value. If you do not fly frequently or do not have the patience to study point redemption strategies, cashback will likely put more money back in your pocket."
  - q: "Do I have to pay tax on cashback rewards?"
    a: "Generally no. The IRS treats cashback as a rebate on spending, not income, so you do not report it on your taxes. The exception is signup bonuses that do not require any spending to earn, though most require at least a minimum spend. When in doubt, consult a tax professional for your specific situation."
  - q: "What is the catch with rotating cashback categories?"
    a: "Rotating categories require you to activate them each quarter, usually through your card's app or website. If you forget to activate, you earn the base rate (usually 1%) instead of the bonus rate. Categories are also capped, typically at $1,500 per quarter in spending for the bonus rate. If you spend more than that in the category, the excess earns only 1%. The discipline required to track and activate is the real cost."
  - q: "Can I have multiple cashback cards?"
    a: "Absolutely. Many people pair a flat-rate card (like Citi Double Cash) for uncategorized spending with a rotating or fixed category card (like Discover it) for specific spending types. The main thing to watch is your total credit utilization across all cards. Adding cards can actually help utilization if managed correctly, but applying for too many cards in a short period creates hard inquiries that temporarily lower your score."
  - q: "What credit score do I need for a cashback card?"
    a: "It depends on the card. The Discover it Secured and Discover it Student Cash Back accept applicants with scores as low as 580. The Citi Double Cash typically wants 670 or above. The Chase Ink Business Cash targets good to excellent credit, usually 700 plus. If you are below 670, start with a student or secured card and work your way up."
  - q: "How does Discover's Cashback Match work?"
    a: "At the end of your first 12 months as a Discover cardholder, Discover automatically doubles the total cashback you earned during that period. You do not need to do anything. If you earned $200 in cashback in year one, Discover deposits another $200 into your rewards account. It applies to the full first year, not just the first statement cycle, so consistent spending throughout the year maximizes the match."
  - q: "Is the Citi Double Cash 2% or 1%?"
    a: "It is effectively 2% but split into two parts. You earn 1% when you make a purchase and the second 1% when you pay that balance. If you carry a balance and never pay it off, you only earn 1%. This is a built-in incentive to pay on time, which is also when the card is most valuable to you financially since you avoid interest charges."
  - q: "What happens to my security deposit on a secured card?"
    a: "Your deposit is held in a separate account and is fully refundable when you close the account in good standing or graduate to an unsecured card. Discover reviews secured card accounts automatically around month 7 and will upgrade qualifying cardholders to an unsecured card and return the deposit. Your credit line on the secured card equals your deposit, so a $500 deposit gives you a $500 limit."
  - q: "Can I use cashback cards for business expenses even if they are personal cards?"
    a: "Yes, but for small business owners it is cleaner to keep business and personal spending separate for tax and accounting purposes. The Chase Ink Business Cash is designed specifically for business use and earns the highest cashback rates on actual business operating expenses. Using a business card also builds your business credit profile separately from your personal credit."
  - q: "How long does cashback take to post?"
    a: "Most cashback rewards post to your account within one to two billing cycles after the purchase. Discover typically posts within the billing cycle of the purchase. Some rewards are only redeemable once you hit a minimum threshold (though Discover's cards have no minimum redemption requirement). Always check your issuer's terms."
