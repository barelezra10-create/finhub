intro: "Business credit cards do three things that personal cards cannot: they build your business credit profile separately from your personal credit, they often come with higher spending limits suited to business cash flow, and they offer bonus rewards categories aligned with actual business spending like office supplies, phone bills, and advertising.

The financial separation argument alone is worth the effort. When you mix business and personal spending on one card, you create an accounting headache at tax time and you miss the opportunity to build a credit profile under your business's EIN. A business with its own established credit can eventually qualify for financing, lines of credit, and vendor accounts without a personal guarantee.

Who should get a business credit card? Any sole proprietor, LLC owner, freelancer, or S-corp operator with regular business expenses. You do not need to be incorporated to qualify for most business cards. Sole proprietors apply with their Social Security number and business name. You just need to have some form of business income or intent.

Who should be cautious? Business cards from American Express and Chase are not covered by the CARD Act, which means issuers can change terms with less notice. They are designed for businesses, not consumer protection. Most do not report to personal credit bureaus unless you default, which is a benefit for keeping your personal score clean but means business card activity does not help personal credit."

faqs:
  - q: "Do I need an LLC to get a business credit card?"
    a: "No. Sole proprietors with no formal business structure can apply for most business credit cards using their Social Security number and their own name as the business name. You simply need some form of self-employment income or business activity. Many freelancers, contractors, and side-hustle operators qualify. Having an EIN (Employer Identification Number) is better for formal separation of finances, but it is not required to apply."
  - q: "Does a business credit card affect my personal credit?"
    a: "Most business credit cards do not appear on your personal credit report as long as you pay on time. American Express, Chase, and Capital One business cards typically do not report to personal bureaus for routine activity. However, if you default or the account goes seriously delinquent, it can affect your personal credit since you signed a personal guarantee on most business cards. Brex is an exception because it does not require a personal guarantee."
  - q: "What is a personal guarantee on a business card?"
    a: "A personal guarantee is your commitment, as an individual, to repay the business card debt if the business cannot. Most business cards include a personal guarantee in their terms. This means the issuer can come after your personal assets if the business defaults. For most small business owners this is standard and unavoidable, but it is worth understanding. Brex and some other corporate cards do not require personal guarantees."
  - q: "Can I earn points on an Ink Business Cash toward travel?"
    a: "Yes, if you also hold a Chase Sapphire Preferred or Sapphire Reserve. Points earned on the Ink Business Cash can be transferred to your Sapphire account and then used for travel bookings at 1.25 to 1.5 cents each, or transferred to airline partners for potentially higher value. This is the essence of the Chase trifecta strategy: using the Ink Cash for 5% categories and the Sapphire for travel redemptions."
  - q: "How much credit does a business card give you?"
    a: "Business card limits vary widely based on your personal credit score, business revenue, and time in business. Most small business cards start at $5,000 to $15,000. Some premium cards do not have a preset limit (they are technically charge cards) and set your spending power based on your payment history. Brex sets limits based on business cash deposits rather than personal credit."
  - q: "Should I use my personal card or a business card for business expenses?"
    a: "Business card is the right answer for almost all business owners. Separation simplifies bookkeeping, prevents audit complications, may offer better rewards on business-specific spending categories, and starts building a business credit profile under your EIN. The only exception is if a personal card offers better rewards on a specific category and you have perfect discipline keeping records separate."
  - q: "What is the difference between a business card and a corporate card?"
    a: "Business cards (like Ink Business Preferred) are issued to small business owners with a personal credit check and often a personal guarantee. Corporate cards (like Brex for enterprise clients) are issued based on business financial metrics and do not require personal guarantees. Corporate cards are designed for companies with multiple employees and centralized expense management. Most small business owners need a business card, not a corporate card."
  - q: "How do I build business credit?"
    a: "Start by incorporating your business and getting an EIN. Then open a business bank account in the business name. Apply for a business credit card and use it consistently, paying in full every month. Apply for net-30 vendor accounts with suppliers who report to business credit bureaus. Over 12 to 24 months of consistent positive history across cards, bank accounts, and trade lines, your business will have its own creditworthiness that lenders can evaluate independently of your personal score."

The right business card depends entirely on where your spending is concentrated. If phone bills and internet are your biggest expenses, the Chase Ink Business Cash earns 5% there with no fee. If you spend heavily across multiple categories with no clear pattern, the Capital One Spark Cash Plus at flat 2% beats trying to optimize.

For travel-focused businesses, the Chase Ink Business Preferred plus a personal Sapphire card creates one of the best combined reward stacks available. Start with the card that matches your current spending profile and add the travel layer once your business credit is established.
